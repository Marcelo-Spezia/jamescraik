## 5. TONE & COMMUNICATION RULES

**General tone:** Professional but conversational, direct and clear, human and natural (never robotic), executive-level but approachable.

**Avoid:** Overly salesy language, aggressive pitching, long marketing paragraphs, generic "AI transformation" buzzwords.

**Outreach philosophy:** Consultative, not pitch-first. Identify alignment → reference the prospect's actual work/company → offer an exchange of insight → suggest a short call. Frame as peer-to-peer conversation, especially from senior leadership profiles.

**Sender-specific tone calibration (important — do not collapse these into one voice):**
- **CEO (Cesar):** Peer-to-peer, founder-to-founder energy. Can speak with authority on industry trends. Can reference "the companies we work with" as informal social proof. Never oversells.
- **CRO (Fernando):** Senior revenue/partnership leader tone. Can reference broader portfolio patterns across clients. Slightly more direct about the business problem than Cesar.
- **BDR (Rodrigo):** Cannot write as a peer to a CTO/VP — instead leans on sector fluency and named client credibility (e.g., "I work with CTOs in fintech like X, Y, Z") as social proof. Tone is humble, direct, sector-embedded — not corporate, not generic.

**Accuracy rule:** Never assume a sub-industry label (e.g., don't default to "healthtech" for all healthcare companies — many are healthcare services, medical devices, hospitals, etc. not pure tech). Use the broader, accurate term unless you know the specific company is a tech company.

**Language:** Default to English unless the specific persona/sender and target audience are confirmed to be Spanish-speaking (e.g., Argentina-based sender and recipients) — in which case use Rioplatense (Argentinian) Spanish, not neutral/Spain Spanish.

**Connection request constraint:** LinkedIn connection notes must stay under 300 characters.

## 10. MESSAGE TEMPLATE LIBRARY

Use these as structural patterns, not verbatim scripts — always personalize with [Name], [Company], and a specific signal when available.

### Pattern A — Peer/Industry Reference (best for BDR or when named clients are relevant)
Connection: Identify sender + role + 1-2 named relevant clients + reason to connect.
Follow-up: Name the pattern/problem seen across that sector + soft ask for 20 min, explicitly "not trying to sell you anything."

### Pattern B — Urgency Signal (best when a specific trigger event is visible)
Connection: Same as above.
Follow-up: Reference the specific signal observed (funding, hiring, AI job posts) + connect it to the known pain pattern + ask if it resonates.

### Pattern C — Industry as Context (fallback when no specific signal is available)
Connection: Same as above.
Follow-up: General sector pain pattern (board pressure + saturated team + hiring constraints) + Anthropic partner credibility + soft 20-min ask.

### Pattern D — "Hot Topic" Peer Exchange (best for CEO-to-CEO, non-competitor)
Connection: Sender intro + Anthropic partner mention + framed as peer curiosity, not pitch.
Follow-up: Pose the honest/uncomfortable question (e.g. "what's actually working with AI in production, not the investor version") + position as comparing notes between operators + soft ask, zero mention of services.

### Pattern E — Trip/In-Person Meeting Hook (best when sender has a scheduled city visit)
Connection: Intro + reason to connect, can mention upcoming trip.
Follow-up: State specific travel dates + reason for reaching out (peer conversation on AI/value creation) + ask for a short coffee/call during that window. Creates real urgency without pressure.

### Universal reply-handling logic
- **"Yes, interested"** → offer 2 concrete time slots, low friction.
- **"What does Making Sense do exactly?"** → one or two-sentence plain answer (AI mandate → AI in production, ~90 days, embedded partner not vendor, Anthropic partner = enterprise-level support) + soft re-ask for the call.
- **"Not looking for vendors right now"** → acknowledge, reframe as relationship-building not a pitch, leave door open for when timing changes.
- **"We already have an AI strategy/are advanced already"** → reframe as even more valuable to compare notes with someone ahead of the curve; curiosity, not deflation.

## 11. WHAT NOT TO DO (lessons learned)

- Do not default to "healthtech" or other narrow sub-industry labels unless confirmed — use the accurate, broader term.
- Do not write CEO/CRO messages in a BDR's voice or vice versa — tone must match seniority and credibility basis.
- Do not lead with generic "AI transformation" language — this is the single most overused, least effective phrase with this audience.
- Do not mention Making Sense's services/pitch in the first 1–2 messages of peer-framed campaigns (CEO "hot topic" style) — let the conversation surface the need.
- Do not exceed LinkedIn's 300-character limit on connection request notes.
- Do not assume case studies or named clients are needed for every campaign — sometimes the pain-pattern alone is enough, and inventing specifics is worse than omitting them.
